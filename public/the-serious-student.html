Who this is for

This program tends to be a strong fit for:

  • • Experienced players who already keep a dedicated practice schedule and want to grow considerably from where they are now.
  • • Beginners, with little or no experience, who are ready to put in consistent work and practice over the long term.
  • • Piano teachers looking to sharpen their own playing and teaching.
  • • Anyone determined to play beautifully and exceptionally, no matter what it takes.

Whichever one you are, it asks the same things of you:

  • • You put in the practice. Real, focused work at the piano most days of the week is where the progress happens. I will show you how to make it count, but I cannot do the hours for you.
  • • You stick with it when it gets hard. Every pianist hits stretches that fight back, and the ones who break through are the ones who keep going.
  • • You are willing to change. Getting better means unlearning old habits and rebuilding them the right way, and that takes more humility than talent.
  • • You're in it for the long term. Real progress takes time, not a few weeks.

Talent is not required, and neither is experience. The students who transform are the ones who are the most committed.

If this sounds like you, I encourage you to apply for an interview with me.

Questions I'm asked

Do you teach adult beginners?

Yes. You don't need talent or experience, just the willingness to work and the dedication to thrive.

I played as a kid and stopped. Can I restart?

Absolutely. What you learned as a kid is still in there somewhere. We rebuild the foundation and put real music on top of it.

How is this different from regular weekly lessons?

Normal lessons give you one hour of help and six days alone. Here, when you get stuck, contact me with your question and I answer with the fix, same day or the next. You're never practicing a mistake for a week.

How much time do I need to practice?

Practice should be a regular part of your day and your week to grow quickly; there is not a set amount, but enough to feel meaningful progress every day.

What does it cost?

As this is a premium program which offers much more personalization and attention, it is priced above standard lessons, with enrollment by the semester. I go over the exact number in the interview.

Why only five students?

To give you my undivided attention so I can focus on your growth.

Do I need a grand piano at home?

No. You need something to practice on, but not a grand. If you don't have one, I'll help you find the right piano. Lessons happen on my Steinway.

In person or online?

In person at my Scottsdale studio, with virtual lessons available when you need the flexibility. The between-lesson guidance works the same either way.
